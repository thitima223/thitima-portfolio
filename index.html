About Me

During my high school years, I had the opportunity to design various parts and complete projects for competitions to build my university portfolio. This experience led me to gain foundational knowledge in 3D part design, electronic circuit design, and basic programming. Upon entering university in this specific field, my interest intensified. I have since actively participated in competition and project design, focusing on mechanical components such as springs, screws, gears, and shafts, alongside 3D Part Design & Assembly using professional CAD software like SOLIDWORKS and Autodesk Fusion 360. In the realm of electronics, I have worked on PCB design and layout in EasyEDA, developed schematics, and studied both Microcontrollers (currently familiar with ESP32, Raspberry Pi 4, Arduino R4) and have begun exploring Microprocessors. My programming experience includes C/C++ development for microcontrollers using the Arduino IDE. For data management and connectivity, I have learned to implement data visualization on Node-RED dashboards and manage various communication protocols, including Wi-Fi, BLE, HTTPS, and MQTT, for efficient data transmission. Furthermore, I analyze and plot collected data using MATLAB . Currently, I am also studying Python on platforms like Colab and Kaggle to explore deep learning applications. So I am particularly interested in the intersection of design and light-based applications. Specifically, I am exploring the Physics of Optical Tweezers (or laser trapping) and its potential applications, such as using deep learning techniques with light data. I am also attempting to simulate Optical Tweezers principles related to atomic physics (though my current knowledge in this area is still quite fundamental)
